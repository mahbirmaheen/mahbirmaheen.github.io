Catastrophic Accidents Caused by Material Failures

MME 313: Crystal Defects, Deformation and Fracture

Presented by: Team Resilience

Introduction

This poster highlights three historic disasters — the Tay Rail Bridge collapse, the Morandi Bridge failure, and the Bhopal Gas Tragedy — focusing on the material failures responsible for each. Understanding these events helps engineers improve design methodology, material selection, and safety regulations.

Case 1: Tay Rail Bridge Disaster (1879)

Synopsis: On December 28, 1879, the Tay Rail Bridge in Scotland collapsed during a storm, sending a train and 75 passengers into the Firth of Tay.

  • Material Failure: Brittle cast iron columns
  • Low fracture toughness
  • Low temperature embrittlement
  • Wind load underestimation
  • Inadequate inspection and testing

Case 2: Bhopal Gas Tragedy (1984)

Synopsis: A leak of methyl isocyanate (MIC) gas from the Union Carbide plant released toxic gas affecting hundreds of thousands.

  • Material Failure: Corrosion in MIC storage tank
  • Runaway exothermic reaction
  • Failure of safety valves and monitoring systems
  • Poor maintenance and cost-cutting measures

Case 3: Morandi Bridge Collapse (2018)

Synopsis: The Morandi Bridge in Genoa, Italy collapsed during rainfall due to structural deterioration.

  • Material Failure: Steel corrosion
  • Concrete degradation
  • Fatigue cracking
  • Environmental exposure and poor maintenance

Engineering Lessons

  • Correct material selection
  • Regular inspection schedules
  • Corrosion protection
  • Structural health monitoring systems
  • Fail-safe design

Conclusion

Material failures such as brittleness, corrosion, and fatigue can lead to catastrophic consequences. These historical disasters improved engineering codes, testing methods, and safety practices. Modern materials engineering now focuses on predictive maintenance, monitoring, and advanced materials to prevent recurrence.
